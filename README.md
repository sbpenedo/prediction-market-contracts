# prediction-market-contracts
Sample prediction-market contracts with resolution rules, settlement sources, and edge-case handling.

## 1. State Regular Gasoline Price (recurring monthly)
Question: Will the [State] statewide average price for regular gasoline reach $X.XX this month?
Rules: This market resolves "Yes" if, on any day from market creation through the final day of the contract month (11:59 PM ET), the [State] statewide average price for a gallon of regular gasoline is equal to or above $X.XX. Otherwise, it resolves "No." Resolution uses the first two decimal places of the reported price only (e.g., a reported $4.617 is treated as $4.61). The resolution source is the American Automobile Association (AAA) state fuel-price page (e.g., https://gasprices.aaa.com/?state=CA), specifically the figure shown under "Regular" in the "Current Avg." row. If AAA does not publish a figure on a given day, that day does not count toward resolution. If AAA changes its page format, URL, or methodology, the market resolves on the closest equivalent published daily state regular-gasoline average. The market may not resolve "No" until 11:59 PM ET on the final day of the contract month.

## 2. Texas Grid Failure (seasonal)
Question: Will the Texas grid fail this winter?
Rules: This market resolves "Yes" if, at any point between December 1, 2026 and March 15, 2027 (11:59 PM ET), the Electric Reliability Council of Texas (ERCOT) declares an Energy Emergency Alert Level 3 (EEA 3) and/or directs transmission operators to shed firm load through controlled (rotating) outages. Otherwise, it resolves "No." For the purpose of this market, "grid failure" means an ERCOT-ordered firm load reduction, whether applied statewide or directed to specific regions. It does not include outages ERCOT did not order, including local distribution outages caused by ice, wind, or equipment damage, nor voluntary conservation appeals, Weather Watches, or EEA Levels 1 and 2. The cause of the emergency (cold, demand, generation shortfall, or otherwise) does not affect resolution. The market resolves on ERCOT's declared action, not on any operating-reserve megawatt figure. The resolution source is ERCOT's official notices and news releases (https://www.ercot.com/). The market may not resolve "No" until 11:59 PM ET on March 15, 2027.

## 3. CS2 Player Kills (per-map prop)
Question: Will [Player] record over [N].5 kills on Map 1 of [Team A] vs [Team B]?
Rules: This market refers to the Counter-Strike 2 match between [Team A] and [Team B] in [tournament/stage], scheduled for [date] at [time]. It resolves "Over" if [Player] records [N+1] or more kills on Map 1, and "Under" if [Player] records [N] or fewer kills on Map 1. Kills recorded in overtime count toward the total. [Player] is the player listed under that handle (or a recognized alias) in the active roster of [Team] on HLTV; name changes or aliases referring to the same player are treated as the same player. Map 1 must be played to completion for the market to resolve "Over" or "Under." If [Player] does not appear in the starting five for Map 1 or plays no part of it, the market is voided and all positions are refunded. If Map 1 is not played at all, is not played to completion, or is decided by forfeit, disqualification, walkover, or default rather than played to a conclusion, the market is voided and all positions are refunded. If Map 1 is remade due to a technical issue, resolution is based on the completed remade map only. If the match is canceled, or delayed beyond 7 days from the scheduled date without Map 1 being completed, the market is voided and all positions are refunded. The resolution source is the official HLTV match statistics page for the map (https://www.hltv.org/), specifically the kills ("K") figure listed for [Player]. Single-player props are gated to top-tier, high-liquidity matches to limit manipulation risk.

## 4. Head-to-Head Rivalry Suite (example: Messi vs Ronaldo, 2026 World Cup)
A market type built around individual rivalries rather than team outcomes, settling on a single official statistics provider (FIFA).

Who scores more tournament goals? Resolves to the player with more goals across the tournament per official FIFA match data. Penalty-shootout goals do not count, consistent with FIFA's treatment of goal tallies. If level, the market is voided and all positions are refunded.
Who records more goal contributions (goals + assists)? Resolves to the player with more combined goals and official FIFA-credited assists. Penalty-shootout goals do not count. If tied, the market is voided and all positions are refunded.
Whose team advances further? Resolves to the player whose national team is eliminated in the later round. If both teams are eliminated in the same round, the market is voided and all positions are refunded.
